The Black Cyber Recruiter was founded in 2020 by a group of young entrepreneuers who wanted to even the playing field.


For years, African American applicants have stood at a disadvantage in the recruiting process due to these three main issues:


1. GPA: Recruiters measured heavily weighted GPA into recruiting decisions. Black applicants tend to have lower GPA on average than the non-black population. Furthermore, research has shown that GPA is not an accurate measure of how well a candidate will do in a position.

2. Recruiting windows are too short. Some are as short as three weeks long leaving the majority of applicants unprepared with resumes, cover letters, and letters of recommendation far from finished.

3. Nontransparency in job applications discourage black applicants from applying. Research has shown that 36% more black applicants will apply when relocation assistance is provided.


We believe that the Black Cyber Recruiter combats these issue and more, giving black applicants a fair shot at earning the career they want and deserve.
